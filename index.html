Professional Profile

Brahim Brahmi (Senior Member, IEEE) received the Ph.D. degree in engineering from École de Technologie Supérieure (ÉTS), Montreal, QC, Canada, in 2019, specializing in nonlinear control and robotics.

He is currently an Assistant Professor with the Electrical Engineering Department, KFUPM, Dhahran, Saudi Arabia, and also a member of the Interdisciplinary Research Center for Intelligent Manufacturing and Robotics. His research focuses on nonlinear and adaptive control, bio-robotics, rehabilitation robots, industrial automation, the Internet of Things (IoT), motion control for nonholonomic and underactuated systems, haptics, autonomous systems, intelligent systems, and machine learning.

Research Interests

My research focuses on the design, control, and intelligent integration of advanced robotic systems with a strong emphasis on human-centered applications, rehabilitation, and industrial autonomy. I combine nonlinear control theory, artificial intelligence, and mechatronic design to develop safe, adaptive, and high-performance robotic systems operating in complex and uncertain environments.

1. Bio-Robotics & Rehabilitation

Wearable robots, exoskeletons for upper/lower limb rehabilitation, and human–robot physical interaction.

2. Advanced Control Theory

Nonlinear, adaptive, and robust control including sliding mode and backstepping for complex robotic systems.

3. Intelligent & Learning Control

AI, Neural Networks, Fuzzy Logic, and bio-signal (EMG/EEG) based human-intent estimation.

4. Haptics & Teleoperation

Haptic interfaces, force-feedback, surgical robotics, and physics-based VR/AR systems.

5. Digital Twin & IIoT

Digital twins for robotics, Cyber–physical systems, and mixed-reality enabled training.

6. Mobile & Autonomous Systems

Collaborative robots (cobots), mobile manipulators, UAVs, and distributed control of robot teams.

7. Biomedical & Surgical Robotics

Surgical training simulators, haptic skill assessment, and safety optimization in medical robots.

8. Energy & Smart Systems

Control of renewable energy, smart grids, and intelligent energy management optimization.

9. Industrial Automation

Industry 4.0, smart manufacturing, fault diagnosis, and flexible modular industrial systems.
